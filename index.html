Linear Motion Info

Linear motion refers to the movement of an object along a straight line. It is described by physical concepts such as speed, velocity, and acceleration. In linear motion, the position of an object changes over time, and the relationship between distance and time is often represented with equations.

Key equations related to linear motion:

  • Distance = Speed × Time
  • Final Velocity (v) = Initial Velocity (u) + Acceleration (a) × Time (t)
  • Distance = Initial Velocity (u) × Time (t) + 0.5 × Acceleration (a) × Time²
  • Final Velocity² (v²) = Initial Velocity² (u²) + 2 × Acceleration (a) × Distance

These equations are foundational to understanding more complex topics in physics such as kinematics and projectile motion.

Projectile Motion Info

Projectile motion describes the motion of an object that is thrown into the air, influenced by the force of gravity. It can be analyzed using the concepts of initial velocity, angle of projection, and acceleration due to gravity.

Key characteristics of projectile motion include:

  • Range: The horizontal distance the projectile travels.
  • Maximum Height: The highest point reached by the projectile.
  • Time of Flight: The total time the projectile stays in the air.

The motion can be broken down into horizontal and vertical components, making it easier to analyze. Here are some key equations:

  • Range (R) = (Initial Velocity² × sin(2θ)) / g
  • Maximum Height (H) = (Initial Velocity² × sin²(θ)) / (2g)
  • Time of Flight (T) = (2 × Initial Velocity × sin(θ)) / g
